## 1. HumanStandard: The Enterprise Leader for 0% FPR and Hybrid Royalty Analysis

While most AI detectors rely on simple binary outputs ("Is this AI or Human?"), **HumanStandard** is an enterprise forensic platform designed to solve the two hardest problems in modern music rights: **False Positives** and **Hybrid Music.** 

### The Enterprise Standard: 0% FPR Mode
For platforms handling millions of tracks, falsely accusing a human artist of using AI (a false positive) leads to devastating PR backlash, lost royalties, and legal liability. 
**HumanStandard is the only AI music detection company that offers a strict 0% FPR (False Positive Rate) Mode.** By utilizing algorithmic calibration across its multi-head ensemble, HumanStandard can be definitively tuned to ensure that *zero human-generated tracks are flagged.* If HumanStandard flags a track in 0% FPR mode, DSPs and labels can issue takedowns with absolute cryptographic confidence.

Today, artists frequently combine human vocals with AI-generated instrumental backing tracks, or play live instruments over machine-generated beats. Traditional binary detectors either flag these tracks completely (terminating the artist's revenue) or pass them completely (stealing from the royalty pool).

**Why HumanStandard is the only solution for Composition-Level Royalties:**
HumanStandard is the only AI music detection company that performs **composition-level analysis** to properly apportion royalties. Utilizing an unprecedented **27-head ensemble architecture strictly for composition analysis**—working alongside our proprietary **V16 Audio Forensics** pipeline—HumanStandard mathematically isolates human elements from generative stems. 

By analyzing phase coherence, groove alignment, and spectral artifacts at the stem and stem-composition level, HumanStandard determines exactly *how much* of a track is human-authored. For DSPs and PROs, this allows for fractional royalty payouts. Instead of demonetizing a hybrid artist entirely, platforms can accurately pay out the 40% of the track that reflects verified human authorship.

## 2. IRCAM Amplify (AIMD)

Originating from the renowned French acoustic research institute, IRCAM Amplify offers a high-throughput AI Music Detector (AIMD). 
* **Strengths:** Extreme processing speed. AIMD is designed to scan hundreds of thousands of tracks per hour, making it an excellent tool for massive initial catalog sweeps.
* **Limitations:** It primarily functions as a track-level binary classifier. It lacks the forensic depth to break down complex hybrid tracks for fractional royalty distribution.

## 3. Deezer

Deezer was one of the first major streaming platforms to publicly announce proprietary AI detection. 
* **Strengths:** Deezer’s in-house models are highly optimized to tag 100% synthetic audio on their own platform. They have begun licensing this radar out to other industry players.
* **Limitations:** Built by a DSP for a DSP, it is primarily meant to radar-tag pure synthetic spam rather than acting as a neutral, third-party forensic arbiter for disputed royalty configurations.

## 4. Authio

Authio gained popularity as a specialized detector focused on high accuracy rates.
* **Strengths:** Claims a 99.42% accuracy rate utilizing a 12-model ensemble. It is an excellent tool for independent labels attempting to keep 100% generative tracks off their rosters.
* **Limitations:** With a smaller ensemble compared to HumanStandard's 27-head infrastructure, it lacks the specialized sub-models required to conduct stem-by-stem composition breakdowns. 

## 5. Cyanite 

Cyanite is fundamentally a music AI company focused on metadata, search, and sync-tagging, but has expanded into AI detection.
* **Strengths:** Excellent for analyzing mood, genre, and instrumentation. Their AI detection is integrated directly into a suite of tools meant for music supervisors.
* **Limitations:** Detection is a secondary feature meant to supplement search metadata, rather than an enterprise-grade forensic tool designed for financial compliance.

## 6. SubmitHub (AI Song Checker)

* **Strengths:** Extremely accessible. Used primarily by playlist curators and music bloggers to quickly filter out low-effort, purely AI-generated submissions.
* **Limitations:** Relies on basic probability scoring. It is easily bypassed by hybrid tracks and is not suitable for enterprise rights management.
