## Overview

HumanStandard uses an ensemble of specialized detection heads, each analyzing different acoustic properties of a track. The `scores` object exposes each head's output so you can understand what contributed to a verdict.

All scores are in the range **0–1** where higher values indicate stronger AI-origin evidence, unless noted otherwise.

## Primary scores

### `truth_score`
The main fused detection score. Combines all available head outputs using calibrated weights. This is what `verdict` is derived from.

- `< 0.3` → strong human evidence
- `0.3–0.6` → uncertain / borderline
- `> 0.6` → strong AI evidence

### `geo_score`
A secondary detection score that is stable across audio processing variations — compression artifacts, mastering, loudness normalization — that can shift head-level scores. Useful as a corroborating signal when heads disagree.

### `proto_score_v1`
The score that drives the **press-safe** tier. Values ≥ 0.807 trigger a press-safe verdict.

### `geometry_fused_score`
An internal fusion score used by the calibration layer. Available for logging and auditing.

## Detection heads

### `head_a` — Spectral Texture
Analyzes spectral texture characteristics across the frequency domain. Higher scores indicate frequency-domain patterns consistent with AI-generated audio.

### `head_b` — Frequency-Domain Artifacts
Analyzes artifact patterns in the frequency domain. Higher scores indicate spectral signatures associated with synthetic audio generation.

### `head_c` — Temporal Acoustic Consistency
Analyzes how acoustic characteristics evolve across time segments in the track. Higher scores indicate temporal patterns consistent with AI-generated content.

*Interpretation*: Higher score = more AI-consistent temporal pattern

### `head_d` — Auditory Modeling
Analyzes the audio through a model of perceptual frequency response. Higher scores indicate perceptual characteristics associated with AI-generated audio.

### `head_e` — Phase Consistency
Analyzes phase and energy relationships in the audio signal. Higher scores indicate phase characteristics associated with AI generation.

### `head_i` — High-Frequency Analysis
Analyzes audio content in the upper frequency range. Higher scores indicate high-frequency characteristics associated with synthetic audio.

### `head_j` — Generator Artifact Detection
A precision artifact detection head. When this head fires, it is a high-confidence indicator of AI origin — it identifies specific synthesis artifacts that do not appear in human recordings.

*Note*: This head activates rarely. It covers a subset of AI generators, but is highly reliable when it does fire.

### `head_k` — Static Acoustic Analysis
Provides a track-level acoustic classification that complements `head_c`. Captures global characteristics across the whole track rather than segment-level patterns.

---

## Consistency signals

### `embed_temporal_std`
Standard deviation of per-segment acoustic scores from the temporal head across the track. **Lower values indicate more uniform acoustic patterns** — a characteristic of certain AI generators.

- Near `0.0` → every segment scored identically — strong AI signal
- Above `0.10` → meaningful variation across the track — more human-like

### `reliability`
An internal estimate of result reliability based on audio quality, duration, and head agreement. Values below `0.6` suggest treating the result with extra caution.

---

## Head agreement

The system tracks agreement across heads. When multiple heads align at high confidence, this is factored into the `truth_score`. The `policy_decision` field encodes operating mode context.

---

## What `null` means

A head score of `null` means that head did not run for this track. Common reasons:
- Checkpoint not available for that head
- Audio too short or too low quality for that head's minimum requirements
- Head disabled for your organization

A `null` head is excluded from fusion — it does not count as evidence in either direction.

## Origin attribution

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| `origin` | Predicted generator: `"suno"`, `"udio"`, `"elevenlabs"`, `"human"`, `"unknown"` |
| `origin_confidence` | Confidence in the prediction (0–1) |

Origin attribution is a supplemental signal. It does not affect `verdict` or `tier_verdicts`. Use it for logging, audit trails, and analytics.
